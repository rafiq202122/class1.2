They most likely found a virtual assistant to take a few things off their plates so they could

What Exactly Does a Virtual Assistant Do?

A virtual assistant , or VA, is an independent contractor or freelancer usually hired to assist
with daily administrative duties and other business services. They're ideal if you're running a
business solo, especially when the workload becomes overwhelming and time-consuming, taking away
opportunities for you to build and grow. A VA is sort of like a personal assistant, but focuses
more on general company tasks.

Virtual assistants work remotely, and responsibilities will vary depending on the skills they
offer and the needs of your business. Typically, VAs will assist with general tasks that do not
require high-level training or knowledge. Here are some of the most common:

Data entry

Scheduling appointments

Managing email accounts

Online research

Social media management

Creating presentations

Bookkeeping

File organization

Customer service

Lead generation

Sometimes, virtual assistants are qualified for more specialized services like graphic design, web
development, and marketing. If you don't have the skills or time, you can find a virtual
assistant who can handle these necessary tasks. Remember that if you hire a VA for creative work,
you'll want to ensure they have the time to complete the more tedious tasks.
